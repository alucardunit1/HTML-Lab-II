Top Safety Features

Rally cars are designed to be much safer than standard production cars. They have a variety of safety features, including roll cages, reinforced bodywork, and special seats and harnesses.

  1. Roll cage - A safety cage protects the driver and co-driver from injury in the event of a crash

  2. Reinforced bodywork - The bodywork is stronger than standard cars, with welded joints and cross bracing in the doors.

  3. Special seats and harnesses - Seats are mounted to reduce the force of a side impact, and harnesses like the HANS (Head And Neck Support) system protect the driver's head and neck.

  4. Fire-fighting system - Automatic and manual fire extinguishers protect the cockpit and engine compartment.

  5. Stronger sidewalls - Rally tires have stronger sidewalls than standard tires to protect them from sharp objects, high-speed cornering, and landing impact.
