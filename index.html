Rally Racing 101

Rally racing is an exhilarating form of motorsport that takes place on closed public or private roads, often in challenging terrains like dirt, gravel, snow, or tarmac. Here's a beginner's guide to understanding the basics of rally racing:

What is Rally Racing

  • Rally racing involves drivers and co-drivers competing in timed stages on varied surfaces and terrains.
  • Unlike circuit racing (e.g., Formula 1 or NASCAR) rally courses are point-to-point, and competitors race against the clock rather than head-to-head.
  • Rallies can last a single day or span multiple days, covering hundreds of miles.

Components of Rally Racing

The Car

  • Rally cars are highly modified production vehicles designed for durability and performance on rough terrain.
  • They feature all-wheel drive (AWD), reinforced suspension, roll cages, and specialized tires for different conditions.
  • Popular rally cars include the Subaru Impreza WRX, Ford Fiesta RS, and Toyota GR Yaris.

The Crew

  • Driver: Controls the car and navigates the course.
  • Co-Driver: Sits in the passenger seat and reads pace notes (detailed instructions about the course) to guide the driver.
  • Service Crew: In rally racing, there is no traditional pit crew like in circuit racing (e.g., Formula 1 or NASCAR), but there is a service crew that plays a similar role.

The Stages/Tracks

  • Normal Stage Rallies are divided into stages (timed sections) and transit stages (non-timed sections where drivers move between the stages)
  • Special Stage A special stage (SS) is a section of closed road at a stage rallying event used for timed speed tests. This is normally a public showcase race where most of the track can be seen from one spot

The Rules

  • Timing: The team with the lowest cumulative time across all stages wins.
  • Pace Notes: Co-drivers use pace notes to describe the road ahead (e.g., corners, jumps, hazards). These are often written during reconnaissance ("recce") runs.
  • Service Intervals: Teams have limited time to repair and maintain their cars between stages.
